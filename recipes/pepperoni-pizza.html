Joey's Pepperoni Pizza

The big one you've all been waiting for. Pepperoni.

Legend foretold it's comming. Ever since man created this illustrious combination, pepperoni and pizza have been combined in a collab that changed the pizza game forever. Behold, our humble entrant into salt, cured-meat heaven.

As per usual, we've included the ingredients and steps you need to cobble together this epic flavor combination below.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of store-bought pizza-dough.
  • 1 jar of store-bought pizza sauce.
  • 1 package of store-bought mozzerella cheese.
  • 1 container of store-bought powdered parmesan cheese.
  • 1 bottle of extra-virgin olive oil.
  • 1 package of old world (Italian-style) pepperoni

Steps

  1. Pre-heat your oven as high as it goes (ideally 500 degrees F). When cooking pizza, less time at higher temp will help achieve a crisper crust, avoid sogginess, and provide more even cooking.
  2. Place your pizza stone inside the oven, letting it pre-heat alongside the oven.
  3. Put your pizza dough in a bowl, and place it in a warm area while your oven pre-heats. This relaxes the gluten structure of the dough, making it easier to roll out.
  4. Once your oven's pre-heated, place some flour on your countertop and roll out the dough into the always classic, circle.
  5. Spread sauce onto your dough, making sure not to use too much.
  6. Place a generous amount of mozzerella cheese on the pizza, making sure there is even distribution.
  7. Place a generous amount of pepperonis on the pizza. Seriously, don't skimp here. Use more than you think you need, as the pepperonis will curl up into cups while cooking.
  8. Dust the top of the pizza with parmesan
  9. Spread some olive oil on the crust, to help achieve ideal browning.
  10. Remove the pizza stone from the oven and put the pizza on it. Immediately place back in the oven.
  11. Cook the pizza for whatever seems like a good amount of time. It won't take long.
  12. Remove from oven, and slice into the triangles we all know and love.
  13. Enjoy!
